How matches are chosen

The tool looks for a clean two-way swap: someone takes your shift on the day you want off, and you take an equivalent shift of theirs on another day, so neither of you changes how many on-calls you do.

  • Like for like. Day swaps with Day, Ward with Ward, and Evening (E) with Evening. N1 and N2 are both nights, so they swap freely with each other.
  • Rest is protected. No one is offered a shift on an LTFT day, a black-outlined protected day, or a rest day straight after their own on-call block.
  • Nights stay legal. A swap is never suggested if it would put either of you over four nights in a row.
  • People only count when on the rota. Someone is only suggested if their rota has started (their cells are filled in) on the dates involved.
  • Best match first. Swaps are ranked by how closely the days off before and after line up, and by having no rest-day warnings.

It’s a suggestion engine — the rota team still makes the final change. Always sanity-check before asking someone.
